High Court of Australia

## Material Facts

- The appellant was the sole director and beneficial shareholder of Trainex Pty Ltd
- Trainex raised over $6 million from investors under deeds requiring the funds to be held on trust for film production
- The appellant applied more then $2 million of these funds for his personal benefit (including purchase of a Gold Coast unit and payments to his loan account with another company)
- He was convicted in the District Court on 5 counts under s 173, [*Crimes Act 1900](https://www.austlii.edu.au/cgi-bin/viewdoc/au/legis/nsw/consol_act/ca190082/)* (NSW) of fraudulently taking or applying property of Trainex for his own use or for a purpose other than that of the company
- The NSW Court of Criminal Appeal dismissed his appeal. He appealed to the High Court

## Issues

1. Whether a director who is the sole beneficial shareholder can be guilty under s 173 of fraudulently applying company property when the company (through him) “consented”
2. The meaning of “fraudulently” in s 173 and the correct jury directions
3. Whether the trail judge’s directions on “claim of right” were adequate

## Rule/Law

- **s 173 Crimes Act 1900 (NSW)**: A director, officer or member of a company who fraudulently takes or applies company property for his own use/benefit or any purpose other than the company’s is guilty of an offence
- A company is separate legal entity. A sole director/shareholder’s self-interested “consent” does not validate fraudulent use of company property
- “Fraudulently” = dishonestly (ordinary standards of decent people). Requires proof the accused knew he had no right to apply the property as he did
- **Peters v The Queen** [1998] HCA 7: Identify the knowledge/belief/intent said to make the act dishonest; jury decides if accused had it and whether the act was dishonest by ordinary standards
- Claim of right: Honest (genuine) belief in legal entitlement negates dishonesty. Prosecution must negative it if raised on evidence

## Application/Analysis

- As a sole controller, the appellant could not give valid consent on Trainex’s behalf to use trust funds for his personal benefit - this breached the deeds and his duties
- Evidence showed he knowingly misapplied investor funds held on trust (false income statements, concealment of records, minimal film production)
- Trail judge’s directions on “fraudulently” (dishonest by ordinary standards) were adequate and followed **Peters**
- No miscarriage from directions on claim of right - evidence overwhelmingly showed no genuine belief in entitlement

## Conclusion

Appeal dismissed. A sole shareholder/director can commit the s 173 offence by fraudulently applying company property.
