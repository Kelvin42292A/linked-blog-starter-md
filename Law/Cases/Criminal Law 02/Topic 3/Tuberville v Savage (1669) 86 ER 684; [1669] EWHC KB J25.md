Court of King’s Bench (UK)

## Material Facts

- The plaintiff put his hand upon his sword and said: “If it were not assize-time, I would not take such language from you.”
- The defendant relied on this as provocation for an assault and battery.
- The plaintiff brought an action for assault, battery, and wounding.

## Issues

Whether the plaintiff’s gesture (hand on sword) combined with his words amounted to an assault.

## Rule/Law

- “The intention as well as the act makes an assault.”
- If words clearly indicate that the person does **not** intend to use force (despite a threatening gesture), it is not an assault.

## Application/Analysis

- The plaintiff’s words made it clear he would **not** draw his sword or attack because the judges were in town (assize-time).
- The conditional words negated any intention to assault at that moment.

## Conclusion

The court held that the plaintiff’s actions did **not** amount to an assault. Judgment was given for the plaintiff.
