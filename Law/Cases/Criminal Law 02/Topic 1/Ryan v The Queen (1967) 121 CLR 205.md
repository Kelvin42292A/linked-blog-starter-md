### Material Facts

- Ryan armed himself with a loaded, cocked swan-off rifle and robbed a service station

- While pointing the gun at the attendant and attempting to tie him up, the attendant moved suddenly

- The gun discharged, killing the attendant instantly

- Ryan claimed the shooting was accidental (reflex action) with no intention to fire

- He was convicted of murder under [*Crimes Act 1900(NSW), ss. 18 (1)](https://anzlaw.thomsonreuters.com/Link/Document/FullText?refType=U5&docFamilyGuid=Ied0f9662d10a11e8a07cfaeacc13e49f&pubNum=1100190&originationContext=document&transitionType=DocumentItem&docVersion=Law+in+Force&ppcid=52c000c395fc435c8003eef436ea0d01&contextData=\(sc.Default\)) and 98.*

### Issue

Whether Ryan’s act of discharging the rile during the armed robbery constituted **murder** or **manslaughter**, particularly whether the shooting was a voluntary act or an accidental reflex action.

### Rule

Under [s. 18](https://anzlaw.thomsonreuters.com/Link/Document/FullText?refType=U7&docFamilyGuid=Ied0f9662d10a11e8a07cfaeacc13e49f&pubNum=1100190&originationContext=document&transitionType=DocumentItem&docVersion=Law+in+Force&ppcid=52c000c395fc435c8003eef436ea0d01&contextData=\(sc.Default\)) (1)(a) of the Crimes Act 1900 (NSW), murder requires a **voluntary act** of the accused causing the death charged

An act done by reflex action on involuntary movement is **not** a voluntary act

In armed robbery cases, the wounding and killing must be caused by the accused’s **voluntary act** for murder to be established (even if there was no specific intent to kill or wound).

### Application

The High Court held that the discharge of the rifle was a **voluntary act** by Ryan. Even if the fatal shot was not intended, pressing the trigger while pointing the loaded gun at the victim during the robbery was a deliberate and conscious act.

The Court ruled that this voluntary act causing death during an armed robbery satisfied the elements or **murder** under [s. 18](https://anzlaw.thomsonreuters.com/Link/Document/FullText?refType=U7&docFamilyGuid=Ied0f9662d10a11e8a07cfaeacc13e49f&pubNum=1100190&originationContext=document&transitionType=DocumentItem&docVersion=Law+in+Force&ppcid=52c000c395fc435c8003eef436ea0d01&contextData=\(sc.Default\)) (1)(a) of the Crimes Act 1900 (NSW). The defence of accident/reflex action failed because the act was not involuntary.

### Conclusion

The High Court dismissed the appeal. Ryan’s act was voluntary, and he was correctly convicted of murder.
