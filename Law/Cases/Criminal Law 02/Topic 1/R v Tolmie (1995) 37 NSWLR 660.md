### Material Facts
- The appellant (Tolmie) and the complainant had been drinking at a club where they were seen kissing, cuddling and fondling each other.
- Later that night, they left the group and were alone on a dark path
- THe complainant alleged that Tolmie forced her to the ground and had sexual intercourse with her without her consent, despite her protests
- Tolmie denied using force and claimed the sexual intercourse was fully consensual
- He was charged with sexual intercourse without consent under [61D of the Crimes Act 1900](https://anzlaw.thomsonreuters.com/Link/Document/FullText?refType=U5&docFamilyGuid=If4b34ea2d10a11e8a07cfaeacc13e49f&pubNum=1100190&originationContext=document&transitionType=DocumentItem&docVersion=Law+in+Force&ppcid=1ebb852e9f0048ab9c702a3367f2b6ea&contextData=\(sc.UserEnteredCitation\))
- The jury convicted him. He was sentenced to 4 years imprisonment with a 2 year minimum term
### Issue
- Did the appellant (Tolmie) commit sexual intercourse without consent under [61D of the Crimes Act 1900](https://anzlaw.thomsonreuters.com/Link/Document/FullText?refType=U5&docFamilyGuid=If4b34ea2d10a11e8a07cfaeacc13e49f&pubNum=1100190&originationContext=document&transitionType=DocumentItem&docVersion=Law+in+Force&ppcid=1ebb852e9f0048ab9c702a3367f2b6ea&contextData=\(sc.UserEnteredCitation\))?
- Whether the sexual intercourse took place **without the complainant's consent**
- Whether the appellant **knew** (or was reckless as to whether) the complainant was not consenting
