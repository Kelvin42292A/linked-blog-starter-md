High Court of Australia
### Material Facts

- Labrador Liquor Wholesales Pty Ltd was prosecuted by Chief Executive Officer of Customs _under Customs Act 1901 (Cth) and [Excise Act 1901 (Cth)](https://anzlaw.thomsonreuters.com/Link/Document/FullText?refType=U5&docFamilyGuid=I8d27be3eee7b11e8af46ddc380776190&pubNum=1100190&originationContext=document&transitionType=DocumentItem&docVersion=Law%20in%20Force&ppcid=3273c91df9cc44fa942d9e11a1542bdd&contextData=\(sc.Default\)&comp=wlau)_

- The prosecutions were brought in the Supreme Court of Queensland

- A major evidentiary issue arose regarding the admissibility of evidence

- The respondent wants to use [Evidence Act, s 92](https://anzlaw.thomsonreuters.com/Link/Document/FullText?refType=U7&docFamilyGuid=I9e94a120039211e99495db3043f758b0&pubNum=1100190&originationContext=document&transitionType=DocumentItem&docVersion=Law%20in%20Force&ppcid=c3991c2f1da64a499c263aea908ba7f4&contextData=\(sc.Default\)&comp=wlau) from [Evidence Act 1977 (Qld)](https://anzlaw.thomsonreuters.com/Link/Document/FullText?refType=U5&docFamilyGuid=I6e246c6f039211e99495db3043f758b0&pubNum=1100190&originationContext=document&transitionType=DocumentItem&docVersion=Law+in+Force&ppcid=c3991c2f1da64a499c263aea908ba7f4&contextData=\(sc.Default\)).

- The proceedings sought conviction of the company

- The company and its directors allegedly failed to pay customs/excise duties on alcohol and cigarettes

- The key procedural question was whether Queensland evidence rules applied and what standard of proof was required.

### Issue

1. **Application of the Evidence Act (Qld)** Does the _Evidence Act 1977_ (Qld) (particularly s 92) apply **of its own force** to prosecutions for offences under the _Customs Act 1901_ (Cth) and the _Excise Act 1901_ (Cth) brought in the Supreme Court of Queensland?
2. **Standard of Proof** What standard of proof is required to obtain a **conviction** for offences under the _Customs Act 1901_ (Cth) and the _Excise Act 1901_ (Cth)?

### Rule

State evidence laws such as [Evidence Act 1977 (Qld)](https://anzlaw.thomsonreuters.com/Link/Document/FullText?refType=U5&docFamilyGuid=I6e246c6f039211e99495db3043f758b0&pubNum=1100190&originationContext=document&transitionType=DocumentItem&docVersion=Law%20in%20Force&ppcid=c3991c2f1da64a499c263aea908ba7f4&contextData=\(sc.Default\)&comp=wlau) cannot apply of **its own force** to proceedings in federal jurisdiction.

However, [Customs Act, s 247](https://anzlaw.thomsonreuters.com/Link/Document/FullText?refType=U7&docFamilyGuid=I5883f1a0ee7011e8af46ddc380776190&pubNum=1100190&originationContext=document&transitionType=DocumentItem&docVersion=Law+in+Force&ppcid=c3991c2f1da64a499c263aea908ba7f4&contextData=\(sc.Default\)) and Excise Act, s 136 provide that prosecutions are to be conducted according to the “usual practice and procedure” of the Supreme Court in **civil cases**. This allows relevant provisions of the state [Evidence Act, s 92](https://anzlaw.thomsonreuters.com/Link/Document/FullText?refType=U7&docFamilyGuid=I9e94a120039211e99495db3043f758b0&pubNum=1100190&originationContext=document&transitionType=DocumentItem&docVersion=Law%20in%20Force&ppcid=c3991c2f1da64a499c263aea908ba7f4&contextData=\(sc.Default\)&comp=wlau) to be applied.

Where the prosecution seeks a **conviction** for an offence against Commonwealth law, the criminal standard of proof-**beyond reasonable doubt**-must be satisfied, regardless of the civil procedural framework.

### Application

The Court held that the [Evidence Act 1977 (Qld)](https://anzlaw.thomsonreuters.com/Link/Document/FullText?refType=U5&docFamilyGuid=I6e246c6f039211e99495db3043f758b0&pubNum=1100190&originationContext=document&transitionType=DocumentItem&docVersion=Law%20in%20Force&ppcid=c3991c2f1da64a499c263aea908ba7f4&contextData=\(sc.Default\)&comp=wlau) did **not** apply of its own force in federal jurisdiction. However, [Customs Act, s 247](https://anzlaw.thomsonreuters.com/Link/Document/FullText?refType=U7&docFamilyGuid=I5883f1a0ee7011e8af46ddc380776190&pubNum=1100190&originationContext=document&transitionType=DocumentItem&docVersion=Law+in+Force&ppcid=c3991c2f1da64a499c263aea908ba7f4&contextData=\(sc.Default\)) and the Excise Act “picked up” the relevant state evidence rules (including s92) because the proceedings followed civil procedure.

Since the prosecution was seeking **conviction** of Commonwealth offences, the criminal standard of proof (**beyond reasonable doubt)** applied, despite the civil procedural framework.

### Conclusion

The [Evidence Act 1977 (Qld)](https://anzlaw.thomsonreuters.com/Link/Document/FullText?refType=U5&docFamilyGuid=I6e246c6f039211e99495db3043f758b0&pubNum=1100190&originationContext=document&transitionType=DocumentItem&docVersion=Law%20in%20Force&ppcid=c3991c2f1da64a499c263aea908ba7f4&contextData=\(sc.Default\)&comp=wlau) does **not** apply of its own force in federal jurisdiction, but relevant provisions (including s 92) are “picked up” via ss 247 of the [Customs Act, s 247](https://anzlaw.thomsonreuters.com/Link/Document/FullText?refType=U7&docFamilyGuid=I5883f1a0ee7011e8af46ddc380776190&pubNum=1100190&originationContext=document&transitionType=DocumentItem&docVersion=Law+in+Force&ppcid=c3991c2f1da64a499c263aea908ba7f4&contextData=\(sc.Default\)) and the Excise Act.

In customs and excise prosecutions, where Crown seeks a **conviction**, the prosecution must prove the elements of the offence **beyond reasonable doubt**.

The appeal was allowed on these point of law.
