New South Wales Court of Appeal

## Material Facts

- The applicant (Elias) secured two significant loans (totalling over $500,000) from commercial lenders by making false declarations about his income and employment status on the mortgage applications.
- The security for the loans (mortgages over real property) far exceeded the amounts borrowed.
- The applicant made all repayment instalments on time and paid all relevant expenses, meaning the banks suffered no actual financial loss.
- He was convicted of making false statements with the intent to obtain a financial advantage under **s 178BB** of the **_Crimes Act 1900_ (NSW)**.

## Issues

- Does obtaining a loan on ordinary commercial terms constitute a "financial advantage" if the loan is fully secured, repaid on time, and the lender suffers no disadvantage?
- Under s 178BB, does the prosecution need to prove the accused had a "dishonest intent"?
- Is the determination of whether something is a "financial advantage" a question of law or a question of fact?

## Rule/Law

- **s 178BB _Crimes Act 1900_ (NSW):** Makes it an offence to make a statement known to be false or misleading in a material particular with the intent to obtain "any financial advantage of any kind whatsoever". _(Note: This section has since been repealed)_
- **Plain Meaning:** The concept of "financial advantage" should be given its simple, plain English meaning.
- **_R v Walsh_ (Applied):** It is completely irrelevant whether the victim (the lender) actually suffered any "financial disadvantage" or loss.
- **_R v Stolpe_ (Applied):** Unlike other fraud offences, s 178BB deliberately omitted the word "dishonestly." The Crown does **not** need to prove dishonest intent; it only needs to prove the accused intended to obtain a financial advantage via a deliberately false statement.

## Application/Analysis

- **Financial Advantage Exists:** The Court held that obtaining the loans clearly provided the applicant with a financial advantage. The loans gave him immediate access to funds he would not otherwise have had at his disposal, allowing him to help his sons.
- **No Disadvantage Required:** The applicant's argument that he shouldn't be convicted because "nobody has been harmed" completely failed. The law does not require the lender to suffer a loss or disadvantage.
- **No Dishonesty Required:** The applicant intentionally signed declarations he knew were false to convince the banks to lend him money. Because the false income figures were highly "material" to the bank's decision to grant the loan, and s 178BB does not require proof of a separate "dishonest intent," the elements of the offence were fully satisfied.
- **Question of Fact:** The Court ruled that whether a loan amounts to a financial advantage is a question of _fact_, not a question of _law_. Therefore, the District Court judge was correct to refuse to submit a "stated case" to the Court of Criminal Appeal .

## Conclusion

The application for judicial review was **dismissed**. The Court of Appeal ruled that obtaining the secured loans constituted a financial advantage and upheld the original conviction.
