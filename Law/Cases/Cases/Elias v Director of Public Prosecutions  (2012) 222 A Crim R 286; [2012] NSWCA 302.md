New South Wales Court of Appeal

## Material Facts

- The applicant obtained substantial loans on ordinary commercial terms from two financial institutions by submitting false declarations of income
- The loans were fully secured by mortgages, with security values far exceeding the amounts borrowed
- All repayment instalments were made on time and the applicant met all relevant expenses - the lenders suffered no financial loss
- He was convicted in the Local Court of making false statements with intent to obtain financial advantage, contrary to [*Crimes Act 1900 (NSW), s 178BB](https://anzlaw.thomsonreuters.com/Link/Document/FullText?refType=U5&docFamilyGuid=I297af6f5d10b11e8a07cfaeacc13e49f&pubNum=1100190&originationContext=document&transitionType=DocumentItem&docVersion=Law+in+Force&ppcid=c9d6e9458b7b4ecaa235977bb98dc84e&contextData=\(sc.Default\)).*
- His appeal was dismissed
- He then sought a stated case to the Court of Criminal Appeal under  [Criminal Appeal Act 1912 (NSW), s 5B](https://anzlaw.thomsonreuters.com/Link/Document/FullText?refType=U5&docFamilyGuid=Ie1452bc6f87c11e8bc3e94cdd1e3c560&pubNum=1100190&originationContext=document&transitionType=DocumentItem&docVersion=Law%20in%20Force&ppcid=c9d6e9458b7b4ecaa235977bb98dc84e&contextData=\(sc.Default\)&comp=wlau) and simultaneously pursued judicial review in the Court of Appeal

## Issues

- Whether obtaining loans on ordinary commercial terms (fully secured by mortgages, with all repayments made on time and no loss to the lenders) constitutes obtaining a **financial advantage** within the meaning of [_Crimes Act 1900 (NSW), s 178BB_](https://anzlaw.thomsonreuters.com/Link/Document/FullText?refType=U5&docFamilyGuid=I297af6f5d10b11e8a07cfaeacc13e49f&pubNum=1100190&originationContext=document&transitionType=DocumentItem&docVersion=Law+in+Force&ppcid=c9d6e9458b7b4ecaa235977bb98dc84e&contextData=\(sc.Default\))
- Whether the question of “financial advantage” in these circumstances is a question of law (for the purpose of a stated case under [Criminal Appeal Act 1912 (NSW), s 5B](https://anzlaw.thomsonreuters.com/Link/Document/FullText?refType=U5&docFamilyGuid=Ie1452bc6f87c11e8bc3e94cdd1e3c560&pubNum=1100190&originationContext=document&transitionType=DocumentItem&docVersion=Law%20in%20Force&ppcid=c9d6e9458b7b4ecaa235977bb98dc84e&contextData=\(sc.Default\)&comp=wlau) (NSW)) or a question of fact
- Whether simultaneously pursuing judicial review and a stated case amounted to an abuse of process

## Rule/Law

[_**Crimes Act 1900 (NSW), s 178BB**_](https://anzlaw.thomsonreuters.com/Link/Document/FullText?refType=U5&docFamilyGuid=I297af6f5d10b11e8a07cfaeacc13e49f&pubNum=1100190&originationContext=document&transitionType=DocumentItem&docVersion=Law+in+Force&ppcid=c9d6e9458b7b4ecaa235977bb98dc84e&contextData=\(sc.Default\))

A person who, with intent to obtain for himself or another person a **financial advantage**, makes a statement (known or believed to be false or misleading) is guilty of an offence

**Key Principles on “Financial Advantage”**:

- Obtaining a loan on ordinary commercial terms can constitute a financial advantage, even if the loan is fully secured and the lender suffers no actual loss.
- “Financial advantage” is assessed from the perspective of the person obtaining it (here, the borrower gains immediate access to funds).
- It is **not** necessary to prove that the lender suffered a financial disadvantage.
- Dishonest intent is not a separate requirement under s 178BB (the intent is to obtain the advantage by the false statement).

[**Criminal Appeal Act 1912 (NSW), s 5B](https://anzlaw.thomsonreuters.com/Link/Document/FullText?refType=U5&docFamilyGuid=Ie1452bc6f87c11e8bc3e94cdd1e3c560&pubNum=1100190&originationContext=document&transitionType=DocumentItem&docVersion=Law%20in%20Force&ppcid=c9d6e9458b7b4ecaa235977bb98dc84e&contextData=\(sc.Default\)&comp=wlau)**

A District Court judge may (and in some cases must) submit a **question of law** arising on an appeal to the Court of Criminal Appeal. Questions of fact are not suitable for a stated case.

**Judicial Review ([Supreme Court Act 1970, s 69](https://anzlaw.thomsonreuters.com/Link/Document/FullText?refType=U5&docFamilyGuid=I13105aecf89311e8bc3e94cdd1e3c560&pubNum=1100190&originationContext=document&transitionType=DocumentItem&docVersion=Law+in+Force&ppcid=c9d6e9458b7b4ecaa235977bb98dc84e&contextData=\(sc.Default\))):**

The Court of Appeal can review District Court decisions for jurisdictional error or error of law on the face of the record. Simultaneous pursuit of judicial review and s 5B proceedings may constitute an abuse of process.

## Application/Analysis

- The applicant obtained a clear **financial advantage** by securing immediate access to substantial loan funds through false income declarations even though:
    - The loans were fully secured by mortgages (security far exceeded loan amounts)
    - All repayments were made on time
    - The lenders suffered no actual financial loss
- Under [*Crimes Act 1900 (NSW), s 178BB](https://anzlaw.thomsonreuters.com/Link/Document/FullText?refType=U5&docFamilyGuid=I297af6f5d10b11e8a07cfaeacc13e49f&pubNum=1100190&originationContext=document&transitionType=DocumentItem&docVersion=Law+in+Force&ppcid=c9d6e9458b7b4ecaa235977bb98dc84e&contextData=\(sc.Default\)),* it is not necessary to prove the lender suffered a financial disadvantage. The advantage is viewed from the borrower’s perspective (access to funds)
- The false statements were made with intent to obtain that advantage
- The question of whether a financial advantage existed was a **question of fact**, not a question of law. Therefore, the District Court judge was not required to state a case under [Criminal Appeal Act 1912 (NSW), s 5B](https://anzlaw.thomsonreuters.com/Link/Document/FullText?refType=U5&docFamilyGuid=Ie1452bc6f87c11e8bc3e94cdd1e3c560&pubNum=1100190&originationContext=document&transitionType=DocumentItem&docVersion=Law%20in%20Force&ppcid=c9d6e9458b7b4ecaa235977bb98dc84e&contextData=\(sc.Default\)&comp=wlau).
- Pursuing both judicial review and a s 5B stated case simultaneously carried a risk of abuse of process

## Conclusion

Appeal dismissed. Conviction upheld.
