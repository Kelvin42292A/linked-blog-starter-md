Court of Criminal Appeal

## Material Facts

- The defendants, together with two other men, broke into a bakery at night
- They attacked and overpowered Scott (the night watchman) and Lai (the maintenance engineer) who were on duty and responsible for the safety of the premises
- The victim were bounded, blindfolded, and had their ankles tied before being locked in a lavatory approximately 30 yards away from the cash office, with locked doors separating them from the safe
- While the victims remained restrained in the lavatory (for over three hours), the defendants broke into the cash office, burned open the safe, and stole £10,474 in cash together with other property.
- The victims could hear sounds of the break-in (breaking glass and heavy banging) but were unable to see or physically intervene

## Issues

1. Whether there was sufficient evidence for the jury to find that the defendants were perpetrators of the robbery with violence at the bakery
2. Whether taking of money from the safe, while the night watchman and maintenance engineer were bound and locked in the lavatory, amounted to robbery (i.e., whether the property was taken “from the person” or “in the presence” of the victims)

## Rule/Law

- The property does not have to be taken directly from the victim’s body. A taking “**in the presence**” of the victim is enough
- “In the presence” includes property that is under the victim’s **immediate and personal care and protection**
- Where a person (e.g. a night watchman) is responsible for guarding property, that property is regarded as being in his presence
- If the accused uses violence or puts the victim in fear **in order to** steal the property, it is robbery even if:
    - The victim is tied up, blindfolded, and locked in another room
    - The victim is some distance away (e.g. 30 yards) and cannot see the actual taking
    - The victim only hears the break-in
- The violence and the stealing are treated as **on continuous transaction**

## Application/Analysis

- Scott (night watchman) and Lai (maintenance engineer) were on duty and responsible for the care and protection of the bakery and its contents, including the money in the safe
- The defendants used violence to overpower, bind, blindfold, and lock both men in the lavatory. This violence was clearly applied **for the purpose of** stealing from the safe
- Although the victims were approximately 30 yards away and locked behind doors when the safe was burned open and the money taken, the property was still under their **immediate and personal care and protection**
- The violence and the theft formed **one continuous transaction**. The defendants had to first overcome Scott and Lai before they could access the safe
- The fact that the victims could not see the actual taking (but could hear the break-in) does not prevent the offence from being robbery

## Conclusion

Convictions restored. The defendants were properly convicted of robbery with violence.
