Court of Common Pleas

## Material Facts

- **The Graduate:** Thomas Bonham was a Doctor of Philosophy and Physic who graduated from Cambridge University. He began practising medicine in London.
- **The Fine and Arrest:** The Royal College of Physicians had a charter (confirmed by Acts of Parliament) that gave them the power to supervise all medical practice in London. The College’s censors examined Bonham, declared him "insufficient" (unfit to practise), fined him, and ordered him to stop.
- **The Prison:** When Bonham continued to practise, the College arrested him and threw him into prison. Under their statute, the College was entitled to keep **half of all fines** they issued, with the other half going to the King.
- **The Lawsuit:** Bonham sued the members of the College for trespass and false imprisonment, claiming they had no lawful authority to lock him up.

## Issues

Can a statute legally authorize an organization (the College of Physicians) to act as both a judge and a receiving party in its own dispute?

## Rule/Law

- **No One Can Judge Their Own Case (_Nemo debet esse judex in propria causa_):** It is a fundamental principle of the common law that a person cannot act as both judge and party in a dispute.
- **Common Law Supremacy over Parliament:** When an Act of Parliament is against common right and reason, or is repugnant or impossible to be performed, the common law will control it and adjudge such an Act to be void.

## Application/Analysis

- **The Conflict of Interest:** By allowing the College to fine Bonham and pocket half the money, the statute essentially made the College both the prosecutor (the party seeking the fine) and the judge (the party deciding if Bonham was guilty).
- **Voiding the Statute:** Because it is absurd and unjust for anyone to judge their own case, Chief Justice Coke ruled that the portion of the Act of Parliament allowing the College to imprison and fine unlicensed practitioners for their own financial benefit was against common right and reason, making it legally void.

## Conclusion

The court ruled in favor of Dr. Bonham, declaring his imprisonment **unlawful**.

### Judges Rules

- **Absolute Impartiality:** No person or government body can ever be the judge of their own dispute. If an institution stands to financially benefit from a penalty it imposes, it has an unlawful conflict of interest.
- **The Birth of Judicial Review:** Parliament is not entirely absolute. If a legislative body passes a law that is absurd, impossible to perform, or directly violates fundamental common law rights, **the courts have the power to declare that law void**.
