### Material Facts

Cheatham stabbed his wife and three-year-old daughter to death, and tried to kill his baby daughter, under a severe delusional belief that he was dying of AIDS and had infected them.

At trial, his lawyer only argued insanity and did not raise the defence of diminished responsibility.

The jury found him guilty of murder and attempted murder.

### Issues

Was the trial judge required to instruct the jury on the alternative defence of diminished responsibility because it was supported by the evidence, even though the defence lawyer did not raise it?

### Rule/Law

The Pemble Principle: A trial judge has an absolute duty to instruct the jury on any defence that is reasonably open on the evidence, regardless of whether the defence lawyer raised, ignored, or expressly abandoned it.

### Application/Analysis

The psychiatric evidence of severe depression, Cheatham’s suicidal actions, and his statement "I just lost control of myself" meant that diminished responsibility was clearly open on the evidence.

The trial judge's failure to instruct the jury on this defence was a miscarriage of justice.

### Conclusion

Appeal allowed, convictions quashed, and a new trial ordered.

#### Judges Rules (Generally)

Rule 1: The Judge's Duty

The trial judge must explain a defence to the jury if there is enough evidence to support it. This duty applies even if the defendant's lawyer forgot to raise the defence, ignored it, or chose to give it up.

Rule 2: The Insanity Test

To be legally insane, a mental illness must make the accused unable to think calmly or sensibly about their actions. Because of this, they cannot understand that what they are doing is morally wrong according to the everyday standards of ordinary people.

#### Judges Rules (Knowledge of nature and quality of act or wrongness)

The Morality Standard: For the insanity defence, "wrong" does not just mean illegal. It means morally wrong according to the everyday standards of ordinary people.

The Reasoning Test: Even if an accused person knows their act is illegal, they are legally insane if their mental illness completely prevents them from thinking or reasoning calmly and sensibly about why the act is morally wrong.
