NSW Court of Criminal Appeal

## Material Facts

- On 21 November 2002, two complainants (JV and LM), both aged **6 years**, were passengers on a school bus in Liverpool, NSW.
- The appellant (Barrass) was driving a car next to the bus.
- While the vehicles were stopped at traffic lights, the appellant **pulled his pants down**, exposed his penis, and **manipulated/stroked his penis** with his hand (described by JV as "wiggling").
- He did this **four times**.
- The appellant was **smiling and laughing** while looking directly at the two girls.
- The distance between the appellant’s car and the girls was **3–6 metres**.
- Both girls could clearly see him, and he could see them.
- The appellant **intended** that the girls see what he was doing.

## Issues

- Does the offence of committing an act of indecency “towards” a child under 10 years (under s 61O(2) Crimes Act 1900 (NSW)) require the act to be committed in the immediate physical presence of the victim?
- What is the correct interpretation of the word **“towards”** in s 61O(2)?
- Is physical proximity (with the ability to see the act) + the accused’s intention that the child see the act sufficient to establish the offence?

## Rule/Law

- For the offence of committing an act of indecency **“towards”** a child under 10 years (old **s 61O(2)** _Crimes Act_):
    - The act **does not** require the offender to be in the **immediate physical presence** of the child.
    - It is sufficient that the act is **directed towards** the child, the child **can see** the act, and the accused **intends** the child to see it.

## Application/Analysis

- The appellant’s act of exposing and masturbating his penis while looking at the two 6-year-old girls and smiling was **directed towards** them.
- The girls were able to **clearly see** the act (distance of 3–6 metres).
- The appellant **intended** the girls to witness his conduct (evidenced by smiling and laughing while staring at them).
- Even though the girls were inside the bus and the appellant was in a separate vehicle, the Court held that **immediate physical presence** is **not required** for the offence of “act of indecency towards” a child.
- The conduct satisfied the **“towards”** element because it was purposefully aimed at the children and visible to them.
- The Court rejected the argument that the act must allow for possible physical contact or occur in the same enclosed space.

## Conclusion

The convictions were upheld. The appellant’s actions amounted to an act of indecency towards the children.
