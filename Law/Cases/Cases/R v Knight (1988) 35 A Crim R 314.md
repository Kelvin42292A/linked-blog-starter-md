Court of Criminal Appeal for New South Wales

## Material Facts

- The appellant made highly abusive and threatening phone calls to a police officer, a magistrate, and a District Court judge after they were involved in his conviction for a prior offence.
- He threatened them and their families with death or bodily injury (e.g., "You're a dead man", "I'm going to shoot you").
- The calls were made from a considerable distance away.
- Based entirely on these phone calls, the appellant was convicted of assault and making false statements giving rise to apprehension for safety.

## Issues

- Whether mere threats of violence made over the telephone constitute an **assault**, specifically whether they create an apprehension of _immediate_ violence.
- Whether a threat of violence qualifies as a **"false statement"** under s 248A of the _Crimes Act 1900_ (NSW).

## Rule/Law

- **Assault** is an act which intentionally or recklessly causes another person to apprehend **immediate** and unlawful personal violence.
- While a threat made over the phone _can_ legally constitute an assault depending on the circumstances, the apprehension of violence must still be immediate.
- A **false statement** (under s 248A) requires a knowingly false claim (such as a fake bomb threat); a mere threat of future violence is not a "false statement".

## Application/Analysis

- **Assault:** The evidence only showed that threats of future harm were made. Because the caller was at a distance, there was no evidence that the recipients were in danger of, or apprehended, _immediate_ violence. Threats that might be carried out "at any time, if at all" do not amount to assault.
- **False Statements:** The statements were purely abusive threats. There was no evidence to suggest they were "false statements" within the meaning of the statute.

## Conclusion

The appeal was **upheld**. Because there was no basis for either the assault or the false statement charges, all convictions and sentences were **quashed**, and acquittals were directed.
