## Material Facts

Burgess attacked a friend with a bottle and a video recorder while she was sleeping. He had no memory of the attack and argued that he was sleepwalking (non-insane automatism).

## Issues

Does violence committed while sleepwalking count as a "disease of the mind" (insanity) or is it a normal state of sleep (non-insane automatism)?

## Rule/Law

A mental disorder is a legal "disease of the mind" if it is caused by an **internal source or weakness** and impairs the mental faculties of reason, memory, or understanding.

## Application/Analysis

Medical experts agreed that Burgess's violent sleepwalking was caused by an internal abnormality of brain function, not an external blow, and had a danger of recurring.

## Conclusion

The appeal was dismissed. Violent sleepwalking is legally classified as a **disease of the mind (insanity)**.

### Judges Rules

- **The Internal/External Test:** The mental breakdown must come from an **internal cause** (such as a sleep disorder, epilepsy, or mental illness) rather than an **external factor** (such as being knocked unconscious by a physical blow).
- **No Brain Damage Needed:** It is a legal term, not a medical one. It does not require physical brain damage or a permanent condition; a temporary mental disturbance is enough.
