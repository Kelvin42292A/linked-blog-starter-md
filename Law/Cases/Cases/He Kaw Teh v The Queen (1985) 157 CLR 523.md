High Court of Australia
### Material Facts 
He Kaw Teh arrived in Melbourne from Kuala Lumpur with 2.788 kg of heroin hidden in his baggage. He was charged under [s. 233B(1) of the Customs Act 1901](https://anzlaw.thomsonreuters.com/Link/Document/FullText?refType=U5&docFamilyGuid=Ifb06d6efee6f11e8af46ddc380776190&pubNum=1100190&originationContext=document&transitionType=DocumentItem&docVersion=Law+in+Force&ppcid=22cfee047923402694d5e9b9d8b05e46&contextData=\(sc.Default\)) of the Customs Act 1901 with:
- Importing a prohibited import (heroin) and
- Possession of a prohibited import without reasonable excuse
### Issue 
Does the prosecution need to prove **mens rea** (knowledge) for these strict liability statutory offences?
### Rule
This is a strong presumption that **mens rea** is required for serious criminal offences unless clearly displaced by Parliament.
- For **importation** [s. 233B(1)(b)](https://anzlaw.thomsonreuters.com/Link/Document/FullText?refType=U7&docFamilyGuid=Ifb06d6efee6f11e8af46ddc380776190&pubNum=1100190&originationContext=document&transitionType=DocumentItem&docVersion=Law+in+Force&ppcid=22cfee047923402694d5e9b9d8b05e46&contextData=\(sc.Default\)), the crown must prove the accused **knew** he was importing prohibited import.
- For **possession** [s. 233B(1)(c)](https://anzlaw.thomsonreuters.com/Link/Document/FullText?refType=U7&docFamilyGuid=Ifb06d6efee6f11e8af46ddc380776190&pubNum=1100190&originationContext=document&transitionType=DocumentItem&docVersion=Law%20in%20Force&ppcid=22cfee047923402694d5e9b9d8b05e46&contextData=\(sc.Default\)&comp=wlau), the crown must prove the accused **knew** of the existence and nature of the prohibited import in his possession. 
### Application
The High Court (majority: Gibbs CJ, Mason, Brennan & Dawson JJ; Wilson J dissenting) held that the presumption of means rea was **not displaced** by the language or purpose of  [s. 233B(1)(b)](https://anzlaw.thomsonreuters.com/Link/Document/FullText?refType=U7&docFamilyGuid=Ifb06d6efee6f11e8af46ddc380776190&pubNum=1100190&originationContext=document&transitionType=DocumentItem&docVersion=Law+in+Force&ppcid=22cfee047923402694d5e9b9d8b05e46&contextData=\(sc.Default\)). The trial judge's direction (that no knowledge needed to be proved) was wrong. The appeal was allowed and a new trail ordered
### Conclusion
Conviction quashed. **Mens rea** (knowledge of the prohibited nature of the import/possession) is an essential element of both offences under  [s. 233B(1)(b)](https://anzlaw.thomsonreuters.com/Link/Document/FullText?refType=U7&docFamilyGuid=Ifb06d6efee6f11e8af46ddc380776190&pubNum=1100190&originationContext=document&transitionType=DocumentItem&docVersion=Law+in+Force&ppcid=22cfee047923402694d5e9b9d8b05e46&contextData=\(sc.Default\)).
