## Material Facts

Bratty strangled an 18-year-old girl to death with a stocking. He claimed a "terrible feeling" and "blackness" came over him and argued he was in a state of automatism caused by psychomotor epilepsy. The trial judge only allowed the jury to consider the defence of insanity (which they rejected), refusing to let them consider separate defences of automatism or manslaughter.

## Issues

If a jury rejects a defendant's insanity plea, can the defendant still rely on a separate defence of automatism, and what evidence is required to allow it to go to the jury?

## Rule/Law

A judge only has to leave the defence of automatism to the jury if the defence lays a **proper foundation** by presenting positive supporting evidence. Furthermore, if the only cause of the involuntary behaviour is a disease of the mind, and the jury rejects insanity, there is no room left for a separate defence of automatism.

## Application/Analysis

Bratty’s only evidence for his blackout was psychomotor epilepsy, which is legally a disease of the mind. Because the jury rejected the insanity defence, and there was no other evidence of an external cause (like a blow to the head or sleepwalking) to explain the blackout, there was no legal foundation to leave automatism to the jury.

## Conclusion

The appeal was dismissed, confirming the trial judge was right to withhold the automatism defence.

### Judges Rules

- **Rule 1: The "Proper Foundation" Test** A defendant cannot get an acquittal simply by claiming "I had a blackout". To raise a defence of automatism (acting unconsciously), the defence **must lay a proper foundation** by presenting positive supporting evidence—usually medical or scientific—showing a genuine possibility that their actions were involuntary.
- **Rule 2: Lord Denning's Definition of "Disease of the Mind"** Any mental disorder that manifests itself in violence and is prone to happen again (recur) is legally classified as a **disease of the mind**.
- **Rule 3: Disease of the Mind Equals Insanity** If the only cause of an involuntary act is a disease of the mind, the defendant **cannot** plead sane automatism for a complete acquittal. They must plead insanity, which leads to secure hospital detention rather than letting a dangerous person go free.
