New South Wales Court of Appeal

## Material Facts

- Accused (electrician) was alone with female complainant in her home while repairing power points.
- He rubbed her leg and back, hugged her, kissed her neck and face, and tried to touch her breast and bottom.
- Complainant did **not** consent and felt scared but did not fight back.
- Magistrate convicted him of **indecent assault** (s 61L _Crimes Act 1900_ (NSW)).
- After the Magistrate convicted the accused (Kennard), he appealed to the Supreme Court, where the judge (Ireland J) **quashed the conviction**.
- The police officer/informant (Fitzgerald) appealing Ireland J's decision to the Court of Appeal to get the original conviction reinstated.

## Issues

- What is needed for "assault" + indecency?
- Does the accused need to know/advert to lack of consent, or is recklessness enough?

## Rule/Law

- Indecent assault = unlawful touching (battery) that is indecent + without consent.
- **Mens rea** (guilty mind): Accused must intend the touching **and** be reckless as to consent.
- Recklessness = "couldn't care less" whether she consented (indifference).
- No need to prove victim was put in fear for every case.

## Application/Analysis

- Touching was clearly intentional and indecent.
- Accused's attitude showed reckless indifference to consent.
- Conviction correct — no separate proof of fear required.

## Conclusion

Appeal allowed. Original conviction upheld. Important case on **recklessness as to consent** in sexual assault cases.
