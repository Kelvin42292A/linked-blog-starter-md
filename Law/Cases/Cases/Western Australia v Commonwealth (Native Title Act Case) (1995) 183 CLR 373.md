High Court of Australia

## Material Facts

- **The Commonwealth Legislation:** On 1 January 1994, the federal Parliament’s _Native Title Act 1993_ (Cth) (NTA) commenced to protect and regulate native title across Australia.
- **The State Legislation:** To counter this, Western Australia passed the _Land (Titles and Traditional Usage) Act 1993_ (WA) (the WA Act) on 2 December 1993. The WA Act purported to retrospectively extinguish all native title in the state and replace it with more limited, administratively defeasible statutory "rights of traditional usage".
- **The Legal Challenge:** Western Australia challenged the constitutional validity of the federal NTA. Simultaneously, Indigenous groups (representing the Wororra, Yawuru, and Martu peoples) challenged the validity of the WA Act for violating federal racial discrimination laws.

## Issues

1. **Survival of Title:** Did native title survive the historical establishment of Western Australia as a British colony?
2. **State Act Validity:** Was the WA Act inconsistent with Section 10(1) of the federal _Racial Discrimination Act 1975_ (RDA) and the NTA, making it invalid under Section 109 of the Constitution?
3. **Federal Act Validity:** Was the NTA a valid exercise of the Commonwealth’s "races power" under Section 51(xxvi) of the Constitution?
4. **The Common Law Question:** Was Section 12 of the NTA—which purported to give the common law of native title the statutory force of a law of the Commonwealth—constitutionally valid?

## Rule/Law

- **Presumption of Survival:** The acquisition of sovereignty by the Crown does not automatically extinguish pre-existing native land rights; native title survives as a burden on the Crown’s radical title unless extinguished parcel-by-parcel.
- **Federal Supremacy (Section 109):** Under Section 109 of the Constitution, if a State law is inconsistent with a federal law, the federal law prevails, and the State law is completely inoperative to the extent of the conflict.
- **Equal Property Rights (Section 10(1) RDA):** State laws cannot selectively strip a specific racial group of their property rights or offer them less security of land enjoyment than other groups in the community.
- **The Races Power (Section 51(xxvi)):** The Commonwealth Parliament has the power to pass "special laws" designed specifically to protect the unique rights and interests of a particular race.
- **Separation of Powers:** The federal Parliament cannot delegate its core legislative power to the courts, nor can it declare that the unwritten, judicially-developed common law has the statutory "force of a law of the Commonwealth".

## Application/Analysis

- **No Global Extinguishment:** Historical records show the Crown did not globally extinguish native title when WA was settled; instead, it intended to clear title on a parcel-by-parcel basis as settlement expanded. Thus, native title survived to the modern era.
- **The WA Act was Discriminatory:** The WA Act selectively destroyed Indigenous native title and replaced it with statutory "rights of traditional usage" that had transparently less security. Under WA’s modified property, mining, and petroleum laws, these traditional usage rights could be overridden by executive discretion far more easily than other non-Indigenous forms of land title. This violated Section 10(1) of the RDA.
- **The NTA is Valid:** The NTA is valid because it acts as a "special law" under Section 51(xxvi) protecting the unique, race-based land rights of Indigenous Australians. It does not impermissibly interfere with WA's basic capacity to function as a State government.
- **Section 12 is Invalid:** Section 12 of the NTA improperly attempted to lock the unwritten, evolving common law of native title into a permanent federal statute. Because the common law changes via judicial decisions without Parliament considering if those changes are "necessary" special laws, Section 12 either failed for lack of constitutional support or because it impermissibly blended legislative and judicial powers.

## Conclusion

The High Court ruled that the **WA Act was completely invalid and inoperative** under Section 109 of the Constitution due to its direct inconsistency with the RDA and the NTA. The **NTA was declared constitutionally valid**, with the sole exception of **Section 12**, which was held invalid but found to be completely **severable** from the rest of the Act.

### Judges Rules

A State legislature cannot bypass federal racial discrimination protections by passing a law that selectively extinguishes native title and replaces it with statutory land usage rights that offer less legal protection and security than ordinary titles. Additionally, under the separation of powers, the federal Parliament cannot pass a statute declaring that the unwritten, judicially-developed common law has the statutory force of a Commonwealth law, as the creation of federal statutory law is an exclusive, non-delegable function of the legislature.
