Court of Criminal Appeal for New South Wales

## Material Facts

- The complainant consumed a large amount of alcohol and fell asleep in the second bedroom while her mother was asleep in the main bedroom
- The complainant awoke feeling a penis penetrating her from behind and saw that it was the appellant.
- The complainant immediately returned home and made a prompt complaint to her flatmate, after which the police were called.
- The jury convicted the appellant and he was sentenced to imprisonment with a non-parole period of seven years and an additional term of three years.
- The appellant’s defence at trial was the complainant was awake, on her knees, and invited him to have sex from behind

## Issues

- Did the judge's instructions accidentally force the accused to prove the victim was awake?
- Did the judge unfairly punish the accused for pleading not guilty and making the victim testify?
- Did the judge miscalculate the length of the sentence under the law?

## Rule/Law

- Whether someone is asleep is a fact, but a sleeping person obviously cannot consent.
- A judge cannot increase a sentence just because an accused person pleaded not guilty and defended themselves at trial.
- The "balance of the term" (extra parole time) cannot be more than one-third of the "non-parole period" (minimum jail time).

## Application/Analysis

- The judge did not make a mistake. Looking at all his instructions, he clearly told the jury the prosecution had to prove she was asleep.
- The judge made inappropriate comments about the accused humiliating the victim at trial. However, this error didn't matter because the judge ultimately did not increase the sentence beyond the standard 7 years anyway.
- The judge did make a math mistake. A 3-year extra term is larger than one-third of a 7-year minimum term. The court fixed this by reducing the extra term to 2 years and 4 months.

## Conclusion

The appeal against the conviction was dismissed, but the **appeal against the sentence was allowed in part**. The sentence was quashed and reduced to a 7-year non-parole period with an additional term of two years and four months.
