Court of King’s Bench (UK)

## Material Facts

- The plaintiff put his hand upon his sword and stated: "If it were not assize-time, I would not take such language from you".
- "Assize-time" meant that the Judges were currently in town.
- An action for assault, battery, and wounding was brought, raising the question of whether the plaintiff's action constituted a provocation/assault.

## Issues

Does a threatening physical gesture constitute an assault if the accompanying words explicitly state that no physical harm will be done?

## Rule/Law

- **The Intent Rule:** Both the physical act _and_ the intention are required to make an assault.
- Physical contact made merely in discourse (such as striking a hand, arm, or breast while talking) without the intent to assault is not an assault.
- Conversely, intending to assault and striking at someone but missing _is_ an assault.
- Holding up a hand in a threatening manner and saying nothing _is_ an assault.

## Application/Analysis

- Although placing a hand on a sword is generally a threatening physical act, the plaintiff's words expressly declared his intention _not_ to assault the other person because the Judges were in town.
- Because the words clearly negated the physical gesture, it proved there was no actual intention to commit an assault at that moment.

## Conclusion

The Court agreed that the conduct was **not** an assault. The plaintiff had judgment.
