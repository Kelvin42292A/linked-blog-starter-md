Privy Council (UK)

## Material Facts

- The four appellants formed a preconceived plan to kill a man in a hut and then fake an accident to escape punishment.
- They got the man partially intoxicated with beer and struck him heavily on the back of the head.
- Believing him to be dead, they took his body, rolled it over a low cliff (krantz), and dressed up the scene to look like an accidental fall.
- However, the medical evidence proved the blow did not kill him; he actually died later from exposure while lying unconscious at the foot of the cliff.

## Issues

**Separation of Act and Intent:** Can the accused be guilty of murder if the act done with _mens rea_ (striking the victim) did not cause the death, but the act that _did_ cause the death (leaving him outside) was done without _mens rea_ because they believed he was already dead?

## Rule/Law

- **The "One Series of Acts" Doctrine:** The court ruled that it is "impossible to divide up what was really one series of acts".
- **Mistake/Misapprehension Does Not Erase Murder:** A crime is not reduced from murder to a lesser crime (like culpable homicide) "merely because the accused were under some misapprehension for a time during the completion of their criminal plot".

## Application/Analysis

- The appellants tried to argue a highly technical defence: they claimed their original intent to kill had ceased by the time they rolled the body off the cliff, meaning the fatal act lacked the necessary _mens rea_.
- The court entirely rejected this separation. Because the accused set out to do all of these actions to achieve their preconceived plan, their actions were viewed continuously.
- The court stated it was "much too refined a ground of judgment" to let the appellants escape the penalties of the law simply because they mistakenly thought they had achieved their goal earlier than they actually did.

## Conclusion

The appeal was **dismissed**. The Privy Council upheld the appellants' convictions for murder.

### Judges Rules

**The Exception:** If you carry out a plan to kill someone and dispose of the body, the law treats everything you do as one continuous act. You are still guilty of murder even if the victim actually dies while you are disposing of them and you mistakenly thought they were already dead from your initial attack.
