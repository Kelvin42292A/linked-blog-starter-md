Court of Criminal Appeal for New South Wales

### Material Facts

The appellant was convicted of indecent assault against two 11-year-old girls.

During a game where each girl sat on the appellant's lap to "steer" his car, he allegedly fondled the breast of the first girl under her clothing, and intentionally placed his hand on the breast of the second girl over her clothing.

The appellant admitted to the car driving game, but completely denied touching either girl's breasts, claiming they fabricated the allegations. He did not claim the touching was accidental.

He was sentenced to 15 months' periodic detention for each count, to be served concurrently.

### Issues

Whether the trial judge misdirected the jury by defining "indecent" as contrary to the standards of morality of "respectable people" rather than the "ordinary person".

Whether the trial judge erred by failing to direct the jury that they had to be satisfied the appellant intended the acts for his own sexual gratification.

### Rule/Law

An assault is "indecent" if it has a sexual connotation and offends community standards of decency. When directing a jury on community standards, terms like "respectable person", "right-minded person", and "ordinary person" all legally have the same import.

If an intentional act is unequivocally sexual (such as touching a female's breast), the motive or intention for sexual gratification is irrelevant. Proof of intention to obtain sexual gratification is only required if the alleged assault is equivocal or ambiguous.

### Application/Analysis

The trial judge did not make a mistake by using the phrase "respectable people"; it correctly focused the jury on maintaining the community's standards of decency.

Intentionally touching an 11-year-old girl's breast for several seconds is unequivocally sexual. Therefore, the judge was completely correct to leave out any requirement for the jury to prove he did it for his own sexual gratification.

### Conclusion

The appeals against both the conviction and the sentence were dismissed. The Court found no errors in the trial judge's directions, and the original convictions and 15-month periodic detention sentences were confirmed.
