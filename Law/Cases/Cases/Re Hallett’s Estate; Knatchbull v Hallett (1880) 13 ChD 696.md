Court of Appeal (Chancery Division)

## Material Facts

- **The Breach:** Mr. Hallett, acting as a trustee and solicitor, misappropriated trust funds by depositing them into his personal bank account, where they were mixed with his own money.
- **The Claim:** Upon Hallett’s death, the beneficiaries sought to trace and recover the trust funds from his mixed account in priority to his general creditors.

## Issues

- Can beneficiaries trace misappropriated trust funds into a trustee's mixed personal account and assert priority over general creditors?
- How are withdrawals from a mixed account legally attributed?

## Rule/Law

- **The Right to Trace:** Beneficiaries have an equitable right to trace misappropriated trust property into its proceeds or substitutes, provided the trust funds remain identifiable.
- **The Withdrawal Presumption:** The court applies a protective presumption to withdrawals from a mixed account, attributing withdrawals to the trustee's personal funds first, thereby protecting the remaining trust money for the beneficiaries.
- **The Right of Election:** Upon tracing, beneficiaries can choose to either claim the original trust property or assert a lien over the mixed fund for a proportionate share.

## Application/Analysis

- **Identifiable Assets:** Hallett’s account still contained funds that were identifiable as trust property.
- **Trustee's Money Spent First:** The withdrawals Hallett made from the mixed account before his death are legally presumed to have been taken from his personal funds first. This kept the remaining trust monies intact, allowing the beneficiaries to trace them and assert priority over Hallett's general creditors.

## Conclusion

The court held that the beneficiaries were entitled to trace and recover the misappropriated trust funds from the mixed account, ranking ahead of general creditors.

### Judges Rules

- **Trust Property Survives Mixing:** Mixing trust money with personal money does not destroy the beneficiaries' rights; they can trace and recover their funds into any mixed asset or account as long as the funds remain identifiable.
- **Fiduciary Money is Spent First:** When a trustee withdraws money from a mixed personal and trust account, the law presumes they withdraw their own money first to protect the beneficiaries' interests.
- **Priority Over General Creditors:** Beneficiaries of misappropriated trust property have an equitable claim that ranks ahead of the trustee's general, unsecured creditors.
- **Flexible Equitable Remedies:** When trust funds are mixed with personal assets, beneficiaries can choose to either claim a proportionate share of the mixed asset or assert an equitable lien over the fund to secure their claim.
