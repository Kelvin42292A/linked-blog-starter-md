Court of Criminal Appeal of New South Wales

## Material Facts

- The victim, a Vietnamese woman, was observed shoplifting items from a K-Mart store at Burwood Westfield.
- The appellant (Aiken) approached her, claimed he had been watching her, and implied he was a security officer.
- He threatened to report her to security unless she “helped” him.
- The victim, believing he was security and feeling scared, followed him to a toilet area.
- In the toilet, the appellant forced her to masturbate him and perform oral sex (she used her teeth, causing pain, so he made her use her hand with soap).
- He ejaculated and then took $200 from her purse.
- The victim later reported the incident to store staff, and police were called.
- Aiken was arrested the same day and made admissions in a recorded interview.

## Issues

- Whether the victim’s consent to the sexual acts was vitiated by the appellant’s threats and deception.
- Whether the appellant’s conduct amounted to sexual assault.
- Whether the taking of $200 from the victim’s purse amounted to robbery.

## Rule/Law

- **Sexual intercourse without consent** (Crimes Act 1900 (NSW) s 61I): A person who has sexual intercourse with another person without the consent of that person and who knows that the other person does not consent is guilty of an offence.
- Consent is not freely given if obtained by threats, intimidation, or deception.
- **Robbery**: Stealing property from a person by the use or threat of violence or by putting the person in fear.

## Application/Analysis

- The appellant's threat (to report the victim to security for shoplifting) was a **non-violent threat** because it did not involve physical force.
- Under the law, a non-violent threat does not cancel out (vitiate) consent for the major sexual assault charge under s 61I.
- The prosecution charged him under the wrong law; they should have charged him under s 65A (sexual intercourse procured by non-violent threats).

## Conclusion

The **appeal was allowed**. Because he was charged under the wrong section, his convictions for sexual intercourse without consent and assault were **quashed** (thrown out) and he was acquitted of those specific charges.
