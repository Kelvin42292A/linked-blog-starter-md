Court of King’s Bench

## Material Facts

- **The Proclamations:** King James I wanted to issue royal proclamations to ban the building of new houses in London and prohibit making starch from wheat, intending to punish violators in his personal court (the Star Chamber).
- **The Consultation:** The King's ministers asked Chief Justice Sir Edward Coke and the judges if the King had the lawful power to create these new offenses by proclamation.

## Issues

Can the King, by royal proclamation, create a new criminal offense that was not previously prohibited by the law of the land?

## Rule/Law

- **No New Offenses by Executive Decree:** The King cannot, by proclamation, create any new offense that was not already an offense under the established law.
- **The Prerogative is Limited by Law:** The King possesses **no prerogative powers except for those that the law of the land allows him**.
- **Proclamations are Only Warnings:** The Executive can only use proclamations to warn or remind subjects to follow existing laws; proclamations cannot create new legal prohibitions.

## Application/Analysis

- **Proclamations Cannot Change the Law:** If an action is not already illegal under the common law or statutes of Parliament, a royal decree cannot make it punishable.
- **No Bypass of Courts:** If an offense is not already punishable by law, the King's proclamation cannot make it punishable in his private executive courts (like the Star Chamber).

## Conclusion

The court held that the King **cannot create new offenses by proclamation** and is strictly limited to the powers granted to him by the law.

### Judges Rules

- **Lawmaking Belongs to Parliament, Not the Executive:** The government (the Executive branch) cannot create new crimes, laws, or penalties on its own whim. Lawmaking is the exclusive domain of the legislature.
- **The Government is Bound by Existing Law:** The executive branch has no "inherent" power to act outside the law. Government actions and powers must always be justified by existing common law or statutory authority.
- **Executive Announcements Are Not Law:** Official press releases, policy announcements, or government proclamations are merely informational. They **cannot alter your legal rights or create new legal liabilities** unless backed by an existing Act of Parliament.
