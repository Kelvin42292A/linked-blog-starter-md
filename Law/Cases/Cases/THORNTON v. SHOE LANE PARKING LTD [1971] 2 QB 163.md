Court of Appeal (Queen's Bench Division) (UK)

## Material Facts

- Thornton drove into an automatic multi-storey car park for the first time.
- A notice outside stated charges and “All Cars Parked At Owner’s Risk.”
- He took a ticket from a machine (which referred in small print to conditions displayed on the premises).
- He parked and later suffered serious injury due to the garage’s negligence.
- The garage sought to rely on an exemption clause printed on the ticket (and displayed inside) excluding liability for personal injury.

## Issues

- Whether the exemption clause (excluding liability for personal injury) was incorporated into the contract of parking.
- Whether the garage gave reasonably sufficient notice of the clause before the contract was formed.

## Rule/Law

- A party seeking to rely on an exemption clause must prove it was incorporated into the contract.
- Incorporation requires that the clause was brought to the other party’s attention by reasonable notice **before** the contract was made.
- Tickets issued by automatic machines are different from those issued by human staff: the contract is typically concluded when the customer inserts money/activates the machine and receives the ticket.
- Conditions printed on a ticket or referred to on a ticket come **too late** if the contract has already been formed.
- More onerous or unusual clauses (especially those excluding liability for personal injury) require particularly clear and prominent notice.

## Application/Analysis

- The contract was formed when Thornton drove in, the light changed, and he received the ticket. The ticket came too late to introduce new terms.
- The small print on the ticket referring to “conditions displayed on the premises” was insufficient notice of a clause excluding liability for personal injury.
- The garage did not take reasonably sufficient steps (e.g., a prominent notice at the entrance) to bring this unusual and wide exemption to Thornton’s attention **before** the contract was concluded.

## Conclusion

The exemption clause was **not** incorporated. The garage is liable for Thornton’s injuries. Appeal dismissed.
