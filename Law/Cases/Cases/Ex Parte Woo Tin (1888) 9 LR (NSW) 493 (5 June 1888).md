## Material Facts

- **The Detention:** Despite a previous court decision (_Ex parte Leong Kum_) declaring it unlawful, the New South Wales Government refused to allow Chinese immigrants to land.
- **The Tax Tendered:** The immigrants had fully paid or offered to pay the required £10 poll-tax under the _Chinese Restriction Act_. Instead of being let free, they were detained by Government authority on board ships.
- **The Court Action:** This was the third time the Supreme Court had to be asked for a writ of _habeas corpus_ to release Chinese immigrants held under these identical circumstances.

## Issues

- Is the Executive Government bound to obey the law as declared by the Courts?
- Is it legal to keep detaining immigrants who have paid their statutory tax, and what are their legal rights while illegally detained?

## Rule/Law

- **Absolute Executive Subjection to Law:** Everyone, no matter how high or low their position, is bound to scrupulously obey the law as declared by the courts. The Executive branch of government is constitutionally responsible for enforcing, not defying, judicial decrees.
- **Payment Ends Detention:** Detaining an individual after they have tendered a legally required statutory fee or tax constitutes **illegal imprisonment**.
- **Right to Escape:** Under the common law, a person illegally deprived of their liberty has a right to repel force by force to defend their freedom. If they must necessarily kill those holding them to escape illegal custody, it is **justifiable homicide, not murder**.

## Application/Analysis

- **Executive Defiance:** The NSW Government knowingly and purposely disregarded the court’s previous legal rulings.
- **Putting Lives at Risk:** By keeping these men illegally confined, the Government was creating an extremely dangerous situation. Because the detention was completely unlawful, the immigrants were legally entitled to violently resist and even kill their captors to escape. The Government was putting the lives of its own officers in extreme jeopardy by enforcing an illegal mandate.

## Conclusion

The Supreme Court granted the _habeas corpus_ order (_rule absolute_) with costs, forcing the immediate release of the immigrants.

### Judges Rules

- **No One is Above the Court:** No government or sovereign has the legal authority to act in open opposition to or disregard of the law once a court has officially declared what that law is.
- **Compliance Erases Custody:** Once a citizen or immigrant meets their statutory duties (such as paying a mandatory tax), any continued detention by state authorities instantly becomes **illegal imprisonment**.
- **Force is Justified Against Unlawful Custody:** If you are illegally imprisoned by the state or private individuals, you have a common law right to use force to regain your freedom. If you are forced to kill those holding you to escape, the law treats this as **justifiable homicide rather than murder**.
