NSW Court of Criminal Appeal (NSW Supreme Court)

## Material Facts

- The appellant (Renee Julie Fuge) was convicted as an **accessory before the fact** to assault with intent to rob whilst armed with offensive weapon
- The principal offenders (RB and Pohl) planned to rob a Hungry Jacks restaurant where RB had recently been dismissed
- RB mentioned in passing that the restaurant “owed her money” and that robbery was the only way to get it; this was treated as a joke by the group
- The appellant was alleged to have assisted by supplying a car key, bandanna, stockings, and a knife and by encouraging the offenders to proceed
- The group conducted reconnaissance and the principals carried out a failed armed robbery at knifepoint
- The trail turned on whether the appellant had actively assisted or encouraged the offence
- She was convicted in the District Court. She sought leave to appeal to the NSW Court of Criminal Appeal

## Issues

1. Whether the trail judge erred in failing to direct the jury on the possible **defence of claim of right** in relation to the principal offence
2. Whether the trail judge erred in failing to give adequate directions regarding a witness’s **motive to lie**

## Rule/Law

**Claim of Right Defence**

- A person who takes property in the honest (genuine) belief that they are legally entitled to it does **not** commit larceny or robbery, even if that belief is mistaken or unreasonable
- This defence applies to the principal offender and extends to accessories who believe the principal has a bona fide claim of right
- Key authorities: Sanders (1990) 57 SASR 102; Langham (1984) 36 SASR 48; Nundah (1916) 16 SR (NSW) 482; Bernhard [1938] 2 QB 264; Lopatta (1983) 35 SASR 101; Walden v Hensler [1987] HCA 54; (1987) 163 CLR 561; Kastratovic (1985) 19 A Crim R 28; Astor v Hayes (1998) 38 A Crim R 219.

**Motive to Lie**

- Where a witness’s motive to lie is raised, the jury must be directed that the accused does not bear the onus of proving the motive, and rejection of the suggested motive does not mean the witness is truthful
- _Jovanovic_ (1997) 42 NSWLR 520 and _Palmer_ [1998] HCA 2 distinguished.
- _Uhrig_ (NSWCCA, October 1996) and _Smith_ [2000] NSWCCA 468 applied.

## Application/Analysis

**Claim of Right Defence**

- The evidence showed only a passing comment by RB that Hungry Jacks “owed her money” and that robbery was “the only way she thought she’d get it.”
- This comment was treated by the group (including Pohl and McFaddyen) as a **joke**
- There was no evidence that RB held an **honest belief** that she was legally entitled to take the money, nor that the appellant believed RB had such a claim of right
- The issue was not sufficiently raised on the evidence, so no direction on claim of right was required

**Motive to Lie**

- The Crown witness (Pohl) had a possible motive to lie (to minimise his own role and gain sentencing benefit)
- However, this was not a central or prominent issue at trial
- The trail judge gave clear directions on the onus of proof and the need for caution with accomplice evidence
- No miscarriage of justice arose from the absence of a more detailed • _Jovanovic_/_Palmer_-style direction.

## Conclusion

Leave to appeal refused. Conviction and sentence confirmed.
