Supreme court of new south wales of criminal appeal

## Material Facts

- The complainant first drank six stubbies of beer, smoked four or five cones of marijuana through a bong and became drowsy.
- The complainant went to a house with Chant and removed her jeans and got into bed with the intention to sleep.
- There is a different version where the complainant said she got to bed and Chant had his penis in her vagina and the complainant told him to go away while Chant did not stop.
- Chant said complainant told him to come with her and he believed the complainant was giving him consent.

## Issues

- Whether the trial judge’s direction on consent was unfair.
- Whether the direction regarding a possible motive to lie invited the jury to speculate that the absence of any motive strengthened the complainant’s account.
- Whether the complainant’s evidence was credible.

## Rule/Law

- **Sexual intercourse without consent** (Crimes Act s 61I): A person who has sexual intercourse without consent and knows there is no consent is guilty of an offence.
- The trial judge must give proper directions to the jury on consent.
- The trial judge must direct the jury with care on any motive to lie by the complainant.

## Application/Analysis

- The trial judge's instructions to the jury were **fair and legally correct**.
- The judge properly handled the defense's argument about the victim's motive to lie. The court confirmed that if a victim has no obvious motive to lie, it is just a neutral fact, not automatic proof that she is telling the truth.
- The judge correctly explained the difference between the victim having lowered inhibitions from drugs/alcohol (where she can still legally consent) versus being so intoxicated that she is unable to **"voluntarily and consciously"** consent.

## Conclusion

The appeals for **both Chant and Madden were dismissed.** Their convictions were upheld because the trial judge did not mislead the jury or cause an unfair trial.
