## Material Facts

Thompson, who was under 18 and mildly intellectually retarded, was charged with murder and armed assault. The Crown argued "felony murder" (killing during a robbery). Thompson wanted to raise the defence of **diminished responsibility**, but the trial judge ruled this defence was not legally available for felony murder. Thompson was convicted of murder.

## Issues

Is the defence of diminished responsibility legally available when a person is charged with felony murder?

## Rule/Law

The defence of diminished responsibility (under s 23A of the Crimes Act) **applies to all forms of murder**, including felony murder. "Mental responsibility" focuses on whether the accused’s mind was answerable for their physical acts and their ability to control their actions. This is a question of degree for the jury to decide.

## Application/Analysis

Because the trial judge wrongly blocked the defence, Thompson was unfairly deprived of his right to present his mental impairment argument to the jury.

## Conclusion

The appeal was allowed, the murder conviction was quashed, and a new trial was ordered.

### Judges Rules

- **The General Rule:** The defence of diminished responsibility is a universal defence that is **always available on any murder charge**, no matter how the murder is legally defined.
- **The Key Test:** Whether an accused's mental responsibility is substantially impaired is a subjective question about their state of mind. It must always be decided by a jury, not blocked by a judge as a matter of law.
