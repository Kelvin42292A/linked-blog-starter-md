Court of King’s Bench

## Material Facts

- **The Claim:** Archbishop Bancroft informed King James I that because judges were merely the King's delegates, the King had divinely granted authority to take any legal case he wished away from the judges and decide it personally.
- **The Objection:** Chief Justice Sir Edward Coke, with the unanimous consent of all the judges of England, openly objected and told the King he could not personally decide cases.
- **The Royal Anger:** The King was highly offended, stating that putting him under the law was "treason to affirm".

## Issues

Does the King have the legal authority to bypass the courts and personally judge criminal or civil disputes?

## Rule/Law

- **No Personal Executive Judgment:** The King cannot personally judge any case. All disputes must be determined solely in an established Court of Justice according to the law and custom of England.
- **The King is Under the Law:** The King is not above the law. As Bracton wrote: _"The King ought not to be under any man, but under God and the law"_ (_quod Rex non debet esse sub homine, sed sub Deo et lege_).
- **Artificial Reason vs. Natural Reason:** Legal disputes are not decided by "natural reason" (common sense). They must be decided by **"artificial reason"**—a highly specialized logic requiring years of study, experience, and adherence to established legal principles.

## Application/Analysis

- **Lack of Legal Training:** Although God endowed the King with excellent natural reason, he was not learned in the specialized laws of his realm.
- **The Safe Shield of Law:** Because the law is the "golden metwand and measure" that keeps the peace and protects the King's own safety, the King cannot strip the courts of their jurisdiction.
- **No Redress Against the King:** If the King were to personally judge or arrest a citizen wrongfully, the citizen would have no legal remedy against him. Therefore, justice must be left entirely to the courts where remedies exist.

## Conclusion

The court declared that the King has no personal power to administer justice; all cases must be solely determined by the Courts of Law.

### Judges Rules

- **The Monopoly on Justice:** The Executive branch (the Crown or Government) has no power to act as a judge. The power to hear, decide, and hand down judgments belongs **exclusively to the courts**.
- **Subjection to the Law:** No ruler, president, or government official is above the law. The rule of law demands that the executive is **strictly subordinate to the law**.
- **Specialized Expertise Wins:** Law is not a matter of political will or common sense. It is a highly technical discipline governed by **"artificial reason,"** meaning political figures cannot override legal precedents with their own personal opinions.
- **No Arbitrary Arrests:** The executive cannot personally arrest or detain citizens, as a citizen must always have a clear legal remedy in court against any wrongful deprivation of liberty.
