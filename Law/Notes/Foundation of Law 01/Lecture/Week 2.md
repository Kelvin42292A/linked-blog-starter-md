### **Australian Consumer Law (ACL) Basics**

- Gift recipients have the exact same rights as purchasers as long as they can provide proof of purchase.
- Consumers cannot return items simply because they changed their mind, if they damaged the goods themselves, or if an unreasonable amount of time has passed.
- If a product has a major, unacceptable fault, the consumer gets to choose whether they want a refund or a replacement.
- Consumers have the right to claim compensation for reasonably foreseeable losses, which can include the transport costs of returning a bulky item like a fridge.
- Any statement or representation made by a seller or manufacturer when trying to sell a product constitutes an explicit guarantee.
- A "consumer" under the law includes purchasers of items ordinarily bought for domestic use, or items that fall under the prescribed monetary threshold of $100,000.

### **Client Management and Evidence Gathering**

- Always clearly identify exactly who your client is right from the start to avoid conflicts of interest, especially if multiple family members are involved.
- Separate witnesses (like a mother and daughter) and interview them individually so they do not influence each other's memories.
- Ask for the exact words spoken rather than a client's conclusions, because "he said, she said" cases ultimately rely on witness credibility.
- Maintain strict objectivity and do not blindly believe your clients, as they may lie or omit facts.

### **Identifying the Correct Defendant**

- You must sue the exact registered legal entity (using an ACN or ABN found on a receipt or an ASIC company search) rather than just a shop's trading name.
- Suing the wrong entity can lead to the case being dismissed, leaving your client liable for the other side's legal costs.
- Depending on the situation, the retailer, the manufacturer, or the importer could all potentially be held liable.
- Be wary of suing "$2 companies" (companies designed to hold no real assets) because even if you win the case, there may be no money to recover.

### **Exam Strategy and Legal Advice**

- Carefully filter the facts of a problem question to separate legally relevant details from irrelevant "red herrings".
- Structure your answers logically by dealing with one issue at a time rather than mixing multiple legal issues together.
