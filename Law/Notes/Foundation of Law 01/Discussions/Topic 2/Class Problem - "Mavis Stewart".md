### The Background

Mavis Stewart, a keen baker, recently renovated her kitchen in Sydney. She visited **Discount Lino Barn (DLB)** with her daughter, Kylie, to replace her hard-to-clean slate tiles with something lower maintenance.

### **The Flooring Issue**

- **The Decision:** Despite Kylie suggesting carpet and a staff member (Mike) suggesting under-floor heating, Mavis insisted on **single sheet vinyl**. She chose a "heavy grade domestic" version.
- **The Usage:** Mavis uses her kitchen for more than just family meals; she runs **cupcake classes** five times a week for 20+ students at a time.
- **The Problem:** After only six months, the floor is badly marked and scuffed, particularly around the island bench where the students stand.
- **The Conflict:** Mavis is refusing to pay the **$45,000** bill because she is unhappy with the quality. DLB is demanding immediate payment.

---

### **The Oven Issue**

- **The Order:** Mavis wanted a specific oven for her cupcakes. She found one in a catalogue from **Cake Cookers** (based in Broome). The picture showed eight slide-out trays designed for patty pans.
- **The Conversation:** Mavis called a staff member named Cathy and explained her specialty recipes. Cathy confirmed the oven had eight trays that held a dozen patty pans each and featured self-cleaning inserts. Based on this, Mavis ordered it.
- **The Problem:** When the oven arrived, the trays were not for delicate Australian patty pans, but were **large American-sized muffin holders**.
- **The Conflict:** Mavis told Cathy the oven was unsuitable. Cathy apologized but said there was nothing they could do. Mavis does not want to pay the **$8,000** for an oven she cannot use for her specific cupcakes.
