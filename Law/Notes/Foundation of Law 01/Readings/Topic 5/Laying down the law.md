# WHAT IS LAW?

Core Definitions

**Dictionary Definition:** Principles and regulations emanating from a government and applicable to a people, whether in the form of legislation, custom, or policies recognized and enforced by judicial decisions.

**Broader Definition:** Engaging with the origins of the purported 'law', specific cultural contexts, characteristics, and theories about how rules and principles are defined.

# AUSTRALIAN INDIGENOUS LEGAL TRADITIONS

**Nature of Traditions:** Each Indigenous Nation living on Country prior to British colonization has its own unique legal system and traditions.

**Communication:** Historically communicated through oral traditions in their own languages, though more recently written down and explained in English by Indigenous writers.

**Persistence:** Indigenous law and customs survived British colonization, persisting today alongside the received British Western legal tradition.

# THE WESTERN LEGAL TRADITION

The Western legal tradition encompasses two primary systems sharing a common heritage: **common law** and **civil law**.

Civil Law System

**Definition:** A legal system based on Roman law, primarily comprised of legislated "codes" with a lesser role for judge-made precedent. Used in continental Europe, South America, and parts of Asia (e.g., Thailand, Indonesia, Japan).

**Methodology:** Civil law rules are generally developed through **deduction** from principles established in codes.

**Hybrid Systems:** Countries like Scotland, Louisiana (USA), and Quebec (Canada) feature mixed legal systems due to historical relationships with both English and French legal legacies.

Historical Evolution

**Roman Law Origins:** Organized by Emperor Justinian into the Corpus Juris Civilis. Following the fall of the Roman Empire, the practice of Roman law ceased in those lands, and written records were temporarily lost.

**11th Century Rediscovery:** Missing Justinian texts were recovered in Italy, becoming the foundation for contemporary civil law by replacing or complementing inadequate local customary laws.

**Divergence:** From this point, common law (developed via English court procedures and decisions) and civil law (developed via Roman law principles, custom, canon law, and royal decrees) evolved separately.

# LEGAL THEORY

**Definition:** Broadly covers the definition, nature, and function of law in modern societies, citizens' obligations, legal reasoning, and the interpretation of legal texts.
