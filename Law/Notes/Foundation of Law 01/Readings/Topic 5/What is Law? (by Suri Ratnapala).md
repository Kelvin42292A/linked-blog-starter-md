**1. Legal Positivism (The Rules)**

Law is defined strictly by what the rules _are_, regardless of whether they are morally good or bad.

- **Key Idea:** Law is simply a set of commands, rules, or norms created and enforced by society or a sovereign government.

**2. Natural Law (The Morals)**

Law must pass a **moral test** to be considered valid.

- **Key Idea:** True laws are based on universal, objective morals—such as divine rules, human reason, or natural human rights. If a law is entirely immoral, it is not a true law.

**3. The Common Law Tradition (The Customs)**

Law is an **evolutionary concept** that grows out of ancient customs and everyday social practices.

- **Key Idea:** Judges do not arbitrarily make up laws; instead, they adapt historical customs, established principles, and past judicial decisions to resolve new disputes.

**4. Legal Realism (The Reality)**

Law is best understood by looking at how it operates in **real life**, rather than just reading formal rulebooks.

- **Key Idea:** Ultimately, the law is whatever the judge decides it is. A judge's final decision is heavily influenced by real-world human factors, including psychology, beliefs, and prejudices.
