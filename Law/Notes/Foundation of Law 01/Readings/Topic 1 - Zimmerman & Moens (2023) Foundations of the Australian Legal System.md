### Public & Private law

Public law deal with governments and individuals or entities while private not include non government issues

### Constitutional law

It’s a law covers basic legal-institutional foundation of a system of government. For example concepts of federal, constitutionalism. Three main type of power are being set out in Australian Constitution are legislative, executive, and judicial. They also include other things like the process to amend the constitution and other things.

### Administrative law

Administrative law limits the power of government. No government department is beyond the reach of administrative law.

### Public International Law

Public international law governs global relations and sovereign issues between states through treaties and customary rules, distinct from private international law which handles domestic cross-border disputes.

### Criminal Law

Criminal law is about violation of a community’s standard of good conduct.

### Taxation Law

A compulsory financial charge (or other type of levy) imposed on individuals and legal entity by government.

### Private law

Private law governs the relationships, rights, and obligations between private individuals, and also applies to interactions between individuals and the state when the state is not exercising special public privileges.

### Contract law

Contract law is about parties contract. There are five main categories:

1. Contractual formation
2. Scope and content of contracts
3. avoidance of contractual obligations
4. performance and termination of contracts
5. remedies of breach of contract

### Tort law

It’s a branch of private law involving “civil wrongs” (not related to contracts) where one person’s wrongful conduct causes harm to another. The goal is to provide compensation (usually money) to the victim.

**The Big three**

- **Negligence:** Failing a “duty of care” (e.g., a car accident)
- **Trespass:** Interference with people, land, or goods (e.g., assault or battery)
- **Defamation:** Damaging someone’s reputation through false statements

### Trusts law

A trust is a **three-party relationship** used to hold and protect assets.

**The Roles:**

- **The Settlor:** The person who give the asset away
- **The Trustee:** The “middle-man” who legally holds and manages the asset
- **The Beneficiary:** The person who eventually gets the benefit/money

**Why use it?**

It is a safety for when the receiver isn’t ready yet, for example being too young or unable to manage the asset themselves.

### Succession law

It manages the legal consequences of death by redistributing property to the right people.

- **If you have a Will:** The law makes sure your instructions are followed (within legal limits)
- **If you have NO Will:** The law uses a “default formula” to distribute your assets to your family

### Property law

It is the “backbone” of the legal system. It defines who owns what and what rights they have over it. It separates **ownership** (legal title) from **possession** (physical control).

### Corporations law

Registration rules for a business. Legal duties of a company directors and the role of members in a company. So, It is the legal framework for how businesses are created, run, and overseen. Main three things are: Formation, Governance and Participation.

### Family Law

It is about when a family breakup and division of everything including **property**, time spending with **children**, child support etc. It covers many areas of law, including equity and trusts, and property.

### Employment relations law

It’s about relations between employers and employees. Also include unions negotiate with employers on behalf of a group of workers.

### Common law

There are two different meaning, as a system of law and as a source of law.

### Common law as a system of law

This refers to a **legal family** or tradition. At first because it was common to the whole county, so it became known as the common law.

### Common law as a Source

This refers to **where the actual rules come from**

### Equity

Equity main purpose is to **“supplements, corrects, and controls”** the common law to **ensure fairness(conscience)**. It it found in **property, contract law and trusts**. It must always prevail when equity and common law has a conflict. Main difference between Common law is that common law punishes/compensates while equity prevents or compels action to “right a wrong”

### Court Case

The judges resolve disputes over **facts or law** through formal hearings. While the court’s power to hear a case is called the **jurisdiction**. In Civil cases a **plaintiff** sues a **defendant** for “relief” (like money or an order). While **Criminal Cases**, the **Crown** (prosecution) takes action against **accused** person.

### Civil jurisdiction

Civil jurisdiction is the power of a court to resolve private disputes where a **plaintiff** seeks legal relief against a **defendant.** Unlike criminal law, it focuses on setting claims between individuals and typically begins with formal **summons** or **statement of claim.**

### Criminal jurisdiction

Criminal jurisdiction is the power of a court to hear cases where the **Crown** (the State) initiates proceedings against an **accused** person for committing an offense. These cases focus on punishing public wrongs and typically begin with an arrest or a **summons** issued to the defendant.

### Appellate jurisdiction

It is the power of court to hear and determine an appeal from the lower court hierarchy.

### Witnesses

A person who gives evidence in a court is called witness.

### Subpoena

If the witness does not come, the court may issue a subpoena, which is a document that orders the person to attend and give evidence or may get arrested.

A subpoena can also be a documents or other material that are evidences.

### Affidavit

It is a writing in a document that is for evidence in court. The witness when signing the affidavit assets (formally declares) that all information provided is true.

### Examination-in-chief

A witness giving evidence must first answer questions by the lawyers representing the party who requested that witness to give evidence.

### Cross-examination

The opposing lawyer ask questions to show that the witness’ evidence is unreliable, untrue or mistaken.

### During the Court

A witness would get Examination-in-chief then cross-examination, then re-examines. Back and forth until gets to the bottom of the truth. This is basic characteristics of common-law system.

### Evidence

It is to prove the existence of fact in issue. Can be either written or oral.

### Written Evidence

Consists of records, deeds, affidavits or other documents

### Oral evidence

Witness personally appearing in court, this is specifically done under **oath** or **affirmation**.
