In “A Fine Balance”, Chief Justice Tom Bathurst argues that legal education must balance three competing pillars to produce effective lawyers:

1. Substantive Law: Maintaining the **Priestley 11** core subjects to ensure graduates can identify legal issues and uphold professional standards
2. Liberal Critique: Teaching critical reasoning and lateral thinking to help lawyers adapt to automation and serve as a “bulwark against populism.”
3. Vocational Skills: Integrating practical tasks (like drafting) into academic units rather than treating them as isolated subjects.

He concludes that universities should focus on “why” and “how” the law works, while **Practical Legal Training (PLT)** should move away from being “university-lite” to focus on real-word application where there are actual “stakes at play”.
