**Aboriginal & Torres Strait Islander Peoples: Identity & Law**

**Diversity & Historical Context**

- **Distinct Communities:** There is no single "undifferentiated" group; there are hundreds of separate communities, each with unique languages, cultures, laws, and traditional lands.
- **Impact of Colonization:** The arrival of the British and subsequent "Frontier Wars" caused devastating population decline, dispossession of land, and loss of culture.
- **Resilience & Demographics:** Despite historical attempts at assimilation, the population has shown incredible resilience. As of the 2021 census, there are over 800,000 Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people (3.2% of the population), with a noticeably younger median age (24 years) compared to non-Indigenous Australians.

**Legal Definition of Indigeneity**

- **No Single Definition:** There is no comprehensive legal definition in Australia, partly due to resistance from First Nations people who have traumatic historical associations with government "certificates".
- **The Standard 3-Part Test (1981):** To access certain rights and entitlements, the government generally requires a person to prove three things:
    1. Proof of descent (in any degree).
    2. Self-identification.
    3. Community recognition (often assessed by local land councils).
- **Native Title Test:** This is a stricter test used for land claims. It requires a person to demonstrate descent from the _relevant_ native title group and recognition specifically by the elders holding "traditional authority".

**Sovereignty & The High Court**

- **Contested Sovereignty:** The British claim to sovereignty over Australia has always been strongly contested by Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples in both fact and law.
- **Protection from Deportation:** In 2020, the High Court (_Love v Commonwealth; Thoms v Commonwealth_) ruled that Aboriginal people who meet the strict Native Title test are not considered "aliens" under the Constitution, meaning they cannot be deported even if they lack formal Australian citizenship.
