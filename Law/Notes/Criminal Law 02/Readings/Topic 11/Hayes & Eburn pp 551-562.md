**1. Criminal Attempt**

- **Core Principle:** Under both New South Wales (s 344A) and Commonwealth (s 11.1) law, the offence of attempt focuses heavily on **criminal intention** rather than the actual outcome of the act.
- **No Defence of Impossibility:** **Physical or factual impossibility is NOT a defence**. If an accused intends to commit a crime but fails because it was physically impossible to complete—such as attempting to import "drugs" that are actually an innocent substance or soliciting a prostitute who is actually an undercover police officer—they are still guilty of attempt.
- **Physical Element (_Actus Reus_):** The accused's acts must be **sufficiently proximate** to the completed crime and **not merely preparatory**. In NSW, the court in _R v Mai_ adopted the test of whether the accused's acts went beyond preparation and **could only reasonably be seen as having no other purpose** than committing the offence. Once this threshold is crossed, **voluntary desistance** (giving up) does not prevent the physical element from being met.
- **Fault Element (_Mens Rea_):** Attempt requires a **specific intention to commit the completed crime**. This is often a higher standard than the completed crime itself; for example, attempted murder requires an _intention to kill_, whereas murder can be proven by _reckless indifference_.

**2. Complicity (Aiding & Abetting)**

- **Knowledge of Essential Facts:** To be found guilty of complicity as an accessory, the prosecution must prove that the accused had **actual knowledge of the essential facts** constituting the principal offence.
- **The Strict Liability Rule (_Giorgianni v R_):** The High Court held that even if the principal offence is one of strict liability (meaning the principal offender can be convicted without proof of intent, such as culpable driving), **an accessory cannot be convicted of complicity without proof of intent and actual knowledge** of the essential facts.

---

**3. Conspiracy**

- **Physical Element (_Actus Reus_):** The core physical element of a conspiracy is the **agreement reached between two or more people** to commit an unlawful act. Conspiracy is a **continuous crime** that exists for as long as the agreement remains in place.
- **Fault Element (_Mens Rea_):** Conspirators must have the **specific intention to carry out the agreed-upon crime** (the unlawful purpose).
