
## **1. Criminal Complicity (Accessory Liability)**

- **Rule:** A principal can be liable even if their agent is not (e.g., drug land/contract killings).
- **Mens Rea:** Requires **actual intention** to assist/encourage.
    - _Giugni v R:_ **Recklessness is not enough** (truck owner knew brakes might fail, but didn't intend dangerous driving).
- **Presence:** Mere presence or passive acquiescence is **not enough**.
    - _Regina v Fan:_ Crown must prove: (1) principal committed crime, (2) accused was present, (3) accused had actual knowledge of facts, and (4) accused intentionally assisted.
- **Liability:** An accessory can be liable for a _more_ serious offence than the principal (e.g., _Howell_ – murder vs manslaughter).

## **2. Joint Criminal Enterprise (JCE)**

- **Rule:** A pre-existing **plan or agreement** to commit an offence. Physical presence is **not** required.
- **Extended JCE (Common Purpose):** If a co-conspirator commits a more serious, unplanned crime, you are liable if you knew it was **possible** (foresight of possibility).
    - _McCauley v Regina:_ Teenage brothers guilty of murder in a path-robbery because they foresaw the possibility of serious harm/death. (No foresight = manslaughter).
    - _Mitchell v The King:_ Mutual embarkation on a crime means everyone is liable. The Crown does **not** need to prove who struck the fatal blow.

## **3. Withdrawal from Complicity / JCE**

- **Rule:** Must be a **timely countermand** with reasonable steps taken to undo previous assistance.
    - _Whiteley v Ridley:_ Sending a cancellation telex too late to stop the "train of events" is not a valid withdrawal.
    - _Regina v Brooke:_ Simply getting "cold feet" and not showing up is **not** withdrawal. You must take active steps (e.g., calling the police or warning the victim).

## **4. Law of Attempt**

- **Rule:** Intending to commit an offence and doing acts **beyond mere preparation** (proximate acts), but failing to complete it (S 344A Crimes Act).
- **Physical Impossibility:**
    - _Regina v Mai:_ Still guilty of attempt even if the crime is physically impossible (e.g., trying to possess heroin that police swapped with plaster), provided you had the intent and took direct action.
- **Attempted Murder:**
    - _Alister v Regina:_ Attempted murder strictly requires **intent to kill**. Recklessness, foresight of probability, or intent to cause GBH is **not** enough.

## **5. Exam Strategy Tips**

1. **Three-Hat Method:** Structure answers using **Prosecution** (offences & elements), **Defence** (counterarguments & defences), and **Bench** (final logical conclusion).
2. **Evidence Gaps:** If mens rea is unclear, state: _"With further evidence as to the mental element, the court may decide X or Y."_ Do not guess.

|Legal Term / Role|What is it? (In Simple Terms)|Pre-Existing Plan Needed?|Must be Present at the Scene?|
|---|---|---|---|
|**Principal (1st Degree)**|The main offender who physically commits the crime.|No plan needed.|Yes, they do the crime.|
|**Principal (2nd Degree)**|An assistant who is at the scene intentionally helping or encouraging.|No plan needed.|**Yes**, must be present. _(Mere presence is not enough__)._|
|**Accessory Before the Fact**|An assistant who helps or encourages the crime **before** it happens.|No plan needed.|**No**, they are absent.|
|**Joint Criminal Enterprise (JCE)**|A group acting together under a shared agreement to commit a crime.|**Yes, required**.|**No**, can be "behind the scenes".|
